Challenge

Kalmarhem offers various shared student accommodations, but keeping them clean has been a challenge. We found that the root issue wasnt just tidinessit was a lack of belonging. Some students saw their stay as temporary, while others faced communication barriers, leading to friction.

Results

To bridge this gap, we took a creative approach. Along with already existing sets of rules and reminders, we filmed a music videofun and engagingto help new residents connect and feel at home. By introducing human elements to the existing communication systems, we fostered social bonds and peer pressure that improved both the living spaces and the experience of sharing them.

Process

User Research: To truly understand the problem, we needed to hear from the students themselves. So, we dove into researchorganizing surveys and focus group discussions to uncover the real dynamics of corridor life.

What we found was clear most residents barely saw each other, many corridors lacked group chats, and yet, when just three or four people sat down for fika, connections formed effortlessly

Through three discussions with ten participants, we listened to their experiences, using their insights to shape a solution that felt natural, inviting, and effective.

User Journey Map: Next, we mapped the current user journey, tracing the everyday experiences of corridor residents to pinpoint where connections broke down and how we could strengthen them.

Empathy Mapping: Next, we created an empathy map, diving into the thoughts, feelings, and behaviors of corridor residents to better understand their experiences and challenges.

System Mapping & competitive analysis : We then created a system map to visualize the entire environment and interactions, followed by a competitor analysis to understand how other solutions were tackling similar challenges.

System Mapping & competitive analysis : After conducting a small-scale test of our music video solution with Kalmarhem, we discovered that new residents began to feel more connected, as if they were becoming part of a shared heritage.

We then conducted a SWOT analysis of our solution, evaluating its strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats to ensure we understand the strengths and limitations of the solution.
