Forest's Gold

The primary goal of this mobile app was to introduce the art of foraging to a generation that might be surprised to learn milk comes from cows.

At the same time, it had to navigate key challenges: preventing over-foraging, educating users on which plants are safe to pick, protecting endangered speciesall while promoting sustainability and eco-social resilience.

Challenge

The biggest challenge was to convert digital interactions into physical, real world actions. We found that, generally younger people in the cities are hesitant to touch grass and interact with natural spaces.

Results

The app features a gamified interface that sparks curiosity and interest in nearby natural parks and forests. We implemented mechanisms inspired by games such as Pokemon GO that promote real life interactions when playing the digital game.

We additionally added digital rewards, level up systems to sustain engagement, interaction and curiosity about forageable plants from the integrated forageable plant library.

Usability Test: We conducted a usability test and discovered:

  • Buttons without text took longer to understand.

  • Some participants only explored the map and profile sections.

  • Most found the foraging filters on the homepage confusing.

Conclusion

In conclusion, refining the UI elements and incorporating a one-time tutorial would be key steps in improving the application for future iterations.
